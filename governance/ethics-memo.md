# Ethics Memo — to the Director

Purpose: To advise on privacy and ethics risks before Copilot go-live.

Issue: Copilot does not create new permissions, but it can make existing access problems easier to find and reuse. Sensitive content could be surfaced from sites that are over-shared, poorly labeled, stale, or accessible through broad groups, anonymous links, or external guests.

This review also does not confirm the Assembly’s compliance with FOIPPA. The scan identifies access, labelling, and sharing risks that may affect privacy, but it does not assess the full legal basis for collection, use, disclosure, retention, or safeguarding of personal information.

Advice: Limit go-live to validated, lower-risk sites. Sites containing personal, HR, audit, legal, financial, security, commercial, or constituency-related information should remain out of scope until the business owner confirms that access, labels, and sharing settings are appropriate.

FOIPPA compliance should be confirmed through the Assembly’s established privacy, legal, records, and business owner review processes before higher-risk sites are included in Copilot scope.

I would not provide a ranked list of the ten individuals who handle the most personal or HR content. This could unfairly single out staff whose roles legitimately require them to work with sensitive information. If there are concerns about user behaviour, use role-based training, manager-supported guidance, and aggregate risk reporting instead.

Staff should also be told that Copilot output is not automatically complete, approved, or authoritative. Outputs must be checked against authoritative sources and handled according to the sensitivity of the underlying information.

Recommendation: Proceed only with sites that have confirmed ownership, appropriate access, current labels, no unresolved sharing risks, and clear user guidance. Defer higher-risk sites until remediation and owner sign-off are complete.
